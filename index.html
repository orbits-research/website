A summary of the ORBITS benchmark. To generate context tasks, we first obtain date ranges for news retrieval starting from Jan. 2019 until the current date. Our news retrieval pipeline takes an indicator, date range, and description as input and generates 10 queries for Google News fetching the top 5 articles for each query for a total of 50 articles. It then filters these articles for relevance, taking the top 5 articles and summarizing these to form the textual context. We use custom prompts for GPT-4o to automate the query generation, filtering, and summarization.


Abstract

Recent years have seen the emergence of large, pre-trained foundation models as a promising approach to time-series forecasting. However, the static and simplistic nature of existing time series benchmarks decreases their utility for real-time forecasting, due to the potential of data leakage between training and testing, as well as the non-stationarity of most real-world time series. Thus, we present ORBITS (Online Real-time Benchmarking of Indicator Time Series), the first truly online benchmarking leaderboard for both unimodal and multimodal (time series and textual context) forecasting models. ORBITS comes complete with an automated, LLM-guided task generation pipeline which enables real-time refreshing as new data and time series become available. These real-time updates challenge models to make forecasts on previously unseen data in a truly real-world context. Our benchmark open-sourced to the public contains over 1021 textual event-based context tasks paired with relevant numerical economic data generated thus far.
